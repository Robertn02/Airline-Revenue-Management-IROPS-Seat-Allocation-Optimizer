Reroute is an open-source constrained-optimization approach to airline disruption recovery. It compares cohort-level seat allocation (LP) against the priority-queue triage gate agents currently use — on the same scenarios, head-to-head, with calibrated risk and explicit constraints.

How it works

Three components, one decision.

A calibrated risk model feeds expected misconnect probabilities into a constrained linear program. The LP solves for the cohort assignment that minimizes total expected cost. No deep learning, no LLM — just optimization done correctly.

1

Risk estimation

A gradient boosted tree (LightGBM) predicts per-passenger misconnect probability from delay, connection buffer, tier, and special service flags. Post-calibrated with Platt scaling so probabilities are usable directly in the LP objective — not just well-ranked.

2

LP allocation

Variables index over (passenger, flight, cabin). Objective minimizes expected loss across the cohort. Hard constraints enforce capacity, MCT feasibility, and a loyalty floor. Solved by SciPy/HiGHS in single-digit milliseconds.

3

Audit trail

Every assignment logs the binding constraints and the cost components that determined it. Inputs and decisions are reproducible, which is what makes the system deployable in regulated environments.

Across 100 scenarios with realistic seat scarcity, the LP allocator produces 18.8% lower expected loss than serial triage at the median. Total avoidable loss reduced: $134,000 across 3,793 simulated passengers.

Risk model AUC 0.92, Brier score 0.058 — well-calibrated probabilities suitable for direct use in the optimizer's objective, not just ranking.

What this is, and what it isn't

This is an open-source exploration of an unsolved problem in airline operations. The synthetic data is calibrated to public sources but is not real airline data. The displacement cost term is simplified. The seat inventory is treated as static during the optimization window.

If you work in airline revenue management or ops control, the things I'd most want to be told about are: how PSS handles inventory updates during a disruption window, whether the cohort framing matches what you actually see, and where my displacement cost simplification breaks in real production traffic.
